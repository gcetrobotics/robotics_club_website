Goal Rush Competition: Rules and Regulations

About: Goal Rush

The robot soccer competition stands as a pinnacle of technological innovation and sporting excitement, where teams from diverse backgrounds converge to showcase the cutting-edge capabilities of robotics. Held amidst an atmosphere charged with anticipation, this event serves as a platform for engineers, programmers, and enthusiasts to push the boundaries of autonomous systems and collaborative algorithms. With teams hailing from various institutions and industries, the competition fosters a spirit of friendly rivalry and mutual learning, as participants vie for supremacy on the pitch.

Design a manually controlled robot capable of playing soccer — striking balls and defending goals.

Arena

Rectangular field with center circle and semi-circular goal areas at both ends.

Robot Specifications

  • Dimensions ≤ 30 × 30 × 30 cm (5% tolerance).
  • Weight ≤ 5 kg.
  • Power ≤ 12V DC.
  • No IC engines, Lego kits, or ball-holding structures.

Teams

  • No team size limit; cross-institution allowed.
  • Only 2 members near arena during matches.

Competition & Judging

  • Round 1: One robot per team; score colored and bonus white balls.
  • Round 2: Two robots (striker + goalkeeper) in 2v2 format.
  • Final round details revealed on event day.
  • Penalties and scoring disclosed during event.

Refer to rule book for more info.
